Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. participating in the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom on August 28, 1963.

Martin Luther King Jr. was born in Atlanta, Georgia on January 15th, 1929. He was a pivotal advocate for African Americans during the Civil Rights Movement in the United States.

We must learn to live together as brothers or we will perish together as fools.
-- Martin Luther King Jr.

He was best known for his role in the civil rights movement and nonviolent protests. His life's work has been honored with a national holiday, schools and public buildings named after him, and a memorial on the National Mall in Washington.

Here's a quick timeline of Martin Luther King Jr.'s life:

  • 1948 - Is ordained as a Baptist minister.
  • 1954 - Becomes minister of Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama.
  • 1955 - Is elected president of the Montgomery Improvement Association.
  • 1956 - King's house is bombed while he is at a meeting. His wife and daughter, home at the time, are uninjured. After the U.S. Supreme Court rules that bus segregation laws are unconstitutional, the Montgomery boycott ends. King emerges as a national civil rights leader.
  • 1957 - The Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) is established in Atlanta, with King as president.
  • 1960 - Moves from Montgomery to Atlanta and becomes co-pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church with his father.
  • 1963 - King is arrested for leading a march in Birmingham, Alabama. While in solitary confinement he writes an essay entitled "Letter from Birmingham Jail." During the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom, King delivers his famous "I Have a Dream" speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial. The demonstration is attended by more than 250,000 people. He is named Time magazine's Man of the Year.
  • 1964 - King stands behind President Lyndon B. Johnson as Johnson signs the Civil Rights Act of 1964 into law. He wins the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1965 - Helps organize civil rights protests in Selma, Alabama. President Johnson signs the Voting Rights of 1965.
  • 1967 - King delivers a speech against the war in Vietnam in New York City. The Poor People's Campaign is launched.
  • 1968 - King is assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee, by James Earl Ray, on April 4.
